1 · Overview

Where the agent sits, and what it owns

The Policy RAG agent is the third of five agents. It receives de-identified, schema-validated clinical facts and answers exactly one question: “Which insurer policy governs this request, and does the documented evidence satisfy that policy's criteria?”

Extractor ──► ICD Coder ──► POLICY RAG ──► Form Filler ──► Critique ▲ ExtractedClinicalFacts (Pydantic-typed, PHI already masked) ▼ PolicyCheckResult (policy_id · match · matched/missing criteria · confidence · explainable rationale)

Two design invariants define the whole architecture:

Invariant 1 — Probabilistic retrieval, deterministic judgment Embeddings and fuzzy matching may influence which policy document is fetched (where tolerance to phrasing helps). Whether that policy's rules are met is decided by fixed rule logic only (where fuzziness would be unsafe). No LLM ever approves or rejects anything.
Invariant 2 — Degrade, never fail Every external dependency (Azure credentials, network, cache file) has a per-call fallback to the fully offline keyword path. A dead API can slow one case; it can never error one.

What happens on every case, in order

Scoring formulas (all deterministic given cached vectors)

retrieval_score = 0.55 × keyword_score  +  0.45 × rescale(best_chunk_cosine, 0.15→0.70)
agent_confidence = 0.3 × retrieval_score  +  0.7 × (criteria_met ÷ criteria_total)

Keyword keeps the majority retrieval share deliberately: it is exact, word-traceable evidence. The semantic term is the tie-breaker that absorbs paraphrase (“knee arthroplasty” ≈ “knee replacement”, “unresponsive to non-operative measures” ≈ “failed conservative therapy”).

5 · Chunking scheme

How a policy becomes searchable chunks

Chunk granularity mirrors the document's own structure, so a semantic hit is always attributable to a specific part of the policy:

// POL-ORTHO-TKR-001 "Total Knee Replacement Medical Necessity Policy" becomes: chunk 1 "…Policy. Procedure covered: total knee replacement, knee arthroplasty, TKR…" chunk 2 "…Policy. Applicable diagnosis: osteoarthritis, degenerative joint disease…" chunk 3 "…Policy. Requirement: Severe osteoarthritis documented" chunk 4 "…Policy. Requirement: Conservative therapy failed for at least 12 weeks" chunk 5 "…Policy. Requirement: Imaging confirms joint degeneration"
  • Policy score = max cosine over its chunks → one strongly-matching requirement is enough to surface a policy.
  • Explainability — the winning chunk is quoted verbatim in the output rationale, e.g. “best-matching chunk: 'TAVR Policy. Requirement: Echocardiogram confirms severe aortic stenosis'”.
  • The title is prefixed to every chunk so short requirement texts still carry their parent-policy context into the embedding.

7 · Security & auditability

Trust properties

  • PHI: masked upstream by the Extractor; re-verified at this agent's entry; only masked text is ever sent to the embedding API.
  • Secrets: credentials live in .env (git-ignored); code reads them via config.py only.
  • Reproducibility: cached vectors make hybrid scores identical run-to-run; criteria verdicts are rule-based; the audit trace records score, mode and rationale per case.
  • Independent verification: Agent 5 (Critique) re-checks that the form's policy_id matches this agent's selection and that the decision is consistent with the criteria outcome.
