How this stays safe and compliant

Privacy by design

Patient name, medical record number, date of birth, address, and phone number are detected and masked in the very first step — before any AI reasoning happens. Every later step re-checks its own input and refuses to proceed if a raw identifier ever slips through.

The AI doesn't guess

Two safety nets, not one: a rare-disease candidate needs 90%+ confidence, and any diagnosis below 70% confidence is sent to a human rather than risk a wrong code — the second rule is what catches a diagnosis the system simply hasn't seen enough of before. The same don't-guess principle applies to conflicting details (e.g. left vs. right side) and incomplete policy documentation.

Full audit trail

Every case produces a timestamped record of what each of the four agents did, what confidence it had, and why — so any decision (or non-decision) can be explained and reviewed after the fact.

Tested against known traps

The evaluation set deliberately includes cases with missing conservative therapy, conflicting laterality, low-confidence rare diseases, unmet policy criteria, and incomplete notes — so the safety mechanisms are proven, not assumed.
